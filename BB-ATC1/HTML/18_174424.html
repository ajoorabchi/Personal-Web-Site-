Title: Recognizing Promoters in DNA Using Bayesian Neural Networks

Author:

Author:

Subject: Qicheng Ma,Jason T. L. Wang Recognizing Promoters in DNA Using Bayesian Neural Networks

Description: Binary data classification is to recognize positive data from unlabeled test data that may contain both positive and negative data. In this paper we propose a two-level approach to recognize E. Coli promoters in unlabeled DNA containing both promoter and nonpromoter sequences. The first-level classifiers include three Bayesian neural networks which learn from three different feature sets. The outputs of the first-level classifiers are combined in the second-level to give the final result. Empirical study shows an excellent performance of the proposed approach. Keywords: computational biology and medicine, data mining and classification, neural networks, promoter recognition. 1 Introduction As a result of the ongoing Human Genome Project, DNA or protein data are accumulated at a speed growing at an exponential rate.
