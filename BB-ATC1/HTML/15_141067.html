Title: A Temporal Executable Action Language

Subject: Dov Gabbay (icstm,Rolf Nossum (adh A Temporal Executable Action Language

Description: This paper presents a temporal language for actions, and shows how to embed some other formalisms from the literature into it. This is intended both to provide access to a unified proof theory within which to compare existing action and planning formalisms, and to provide a convenient common avenue for further research. 246 MEDLAR II Deliverable V.2--4 1 Introduction Current logical formalizations of action exhibit the frame problem, and solving it is a major challenge in AI. This has led to many formalisms for nonmonotonic reasoning, and in particular to techniques based on logical minimization, such as circumscription [7] and Reiter's minimization technichue [8]. We describe another approach to dealing with what persists versus what changes as a result of performing an action.
