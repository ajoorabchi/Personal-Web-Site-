Title: Managing Communicative Intentions in Dialogue Using a Collaborative Problem-Solving Model

Author:

Subject: Nate Blaylock Managing Communicative Intentions in Dialogue Using a Collaborative Problem-Solving Model

Description: In order to understand natural language, it is necessary to understand the intentions behind it, (i.e., why an utterance was spoken). We model dialogue as collaboration between agents. Communicative intentions can then be seen as how an agent is trying to a#ect the collaboration. Most previous work on intention-recognition approaches to dialogue has focused on only a small subset of agent collaboration paradigms (i.e., master-slave), and thus is unable to account for dialogues in other paradigms, such as mixed-initiative collaboration. Previous work has also either modeled dialogues where the agents are only planning or dialogues where agents are only acting. This restricts dialogue-model coverage to only those cases and does not model dialogues where collaboration about acting and planning occurs.
