Title: Algebraic Software Architecture Reconfiguration

Author:

Author:

Subject: Michel Wermelinger,Jose Luiz Fiadeiro Algebraic Software Architecture Reconfiguration

Description: The ability of reconfiguring software architectures in order to adapt them to new requirements or a changing environment has been of growing interest, but there is still not much formal work in the area. Most existing approaches deal with run-time changes in a deficient way. The language to express computations is often at a very low level of specification, and the integration of two different formalisms for the computations and reconfigurations require sometimes substantial changes. To address these problems, we propose a uniform algebraic approach with the following characteristics. 1. Components are written in a high-level program design language with the usual notion of state. 2. The approach combines two existing frameworks -- one to specify architectures, the other to rewrite labelled graphs -- just through small additions to either of them. 3.
