Subject: Noboru Murata,Shuji Yoshizawa,Shun-ichi Amari Network Information Criterion - Determining the Number of Hidden Units for an Artificial Neural Network Model

Description: The problem of model selection or determination of the number of hidden units is elucidated by the statistical approach, by generalizing Akaike's information criterion (AIC) to be applicable to unfaithful (i.e., unrealizable) models with general loss criteria including regularization terms. The relation between the training error and the generalization error is studied in terms of the number of the training examples and the complexity of a network which reduces to the number of parameters in the ordinary statistical theory of the AIC.
