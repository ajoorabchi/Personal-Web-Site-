Title: Real-Time Support in General Purpose Operating Systems

Author:

Subject: Kartik Gopalan Real-Time Support in General Purpose Operating Systems

Description: The growth in the number of different types of real-time applications, i.e, applications with timing constraints on their execution, has led to much rethinking of the design of timesharing multiuser operating systems. This growth has motivated enormous research efforts in the area of real-time operating system design. The principal themes of these research efforts have been to build effective architectures for real-time operating systems, to explore various real-time scheduling algorithms, and to devise effcient resource allocation mechanisms for various system resources. The goal of this report is to present the fundamental design issues in supporting real-time capabilities in a general purpose operating system. We survey representative works in this field and propose an integrated framework for effcient multi-resource allocation and scheduling.
