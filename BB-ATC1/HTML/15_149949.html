Title: Automatic Detection of Omissions in Translations

Author:

Subject: I. Dan Melamed Automatic Detection of Omissions in Translations

Description: ADOMIT is an algorithm for Automatic Detection of OMIssions in Translations. The algorithm relies solely on geometric analysis of bitext maps and uses no linguistic information. This property allows it to deal equally well with omissions that do not correspond to linguistic units, such as might result from word-processing mishaps. ADOMIT has proven itself by discovering many errors in a handconstructed gold standard for evaluating bitext mapping algorithms. Quantitative evaluation on simulated omissions showed that, even with today's poor bitext mapping technology, ADOMIT is a valuable quality control tool for translators and translation bureaus. 1 Introduction Omissions in translations arise in several ways. A tired translator can accidentally skip a sentence or a paragraph in a large text. Pressing a wrong key can cause a word processing system to delete several lines without warning.
