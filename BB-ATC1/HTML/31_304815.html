Title: Continual Planning with Time-Oriented, Skeletal Plans

Author:

Author:

Subject: Silvia Miksch,Andreas Seyfang Continual Planning with Time-Oriented, Skeletal Plans

Description: . In dynamically changing environments a planning system does not have all the required information at the first place and the world state can change, rendering the original plan invalid. Consequently, taking planning to complex, real-world domains calls for close coupling of planning and plan execution, known as continual planning. In domains where a high degree of adaptability is crucial and little a priori knowledge is available, explorative approaches are suitable. In other domains where comprehensive knowledge is available and human life depends on plan execution, like in medical treatment planning, only approved and validated procedures are admitted. Even more, these procedures constitute a rich asset in the planning task. To combine the utilization of such knowledge with the flexibility necessary in real-world applications, the Asgaard system integrates timeoriented, skeletal planning with real-time monitoring.
