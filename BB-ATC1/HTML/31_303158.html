Hierarchical Levels of Detail to Accelerate the Rendering of Large Static and Dynamic Polygonal Environments (Under the direction of Dinesh Manocha) Interactive visualization of large three-dimensional polygonal datasets is an important topic in the field of computer graphics and scientific visualization. These datasets can be static, such as many walkthrough applications, or dynamic, such as CAD scenarios where a designer moves, adds, or deletes parts. In many cases, the number of primitives in these models overwhelms the rendering performance of current graphics systems. In order to view these environments in real-time, approximation techniques must augment the capabilities of the hardware. One method for accelerating the rendering of these environments is polygonal simplification.
