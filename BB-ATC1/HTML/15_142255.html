Title: Digital Signatures and Public-Key Cryptography

Author:

Subject: Simon Blake-wilson Digital Signatures and Public-Key Cryptography

Description: Introduction Written signatures have been used for hundreds of years. Formally, giving your signature to a document provides the following services: integrity ---the message cannot be altered after it is signed. proof of origin ---anyone reading the document is sure you signed it. non-repudiation ---at a later date, you cannot deny signing it. The recipient of your signed document is assured of these services by the fact that your pen flourish is unique, and hard to forge. Twenty years ago, the increased use of computer-based systems led to the need for a digital analogue of written signatures. In [9], Diffie and Hellman introduced the concept of digital signature, which they proposed to achieve using their revolutionary idea of public-key cryptography.
