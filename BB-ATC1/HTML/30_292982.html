Title: Mesh Compression and Its Software and Hardware Applications

Author:

Subject: Tulika Mitra Mesh Compression and Its Software and Hardware Applications

Description: Large 3D graphics models are becoming pervasive with the introduction of WWW and virtual-reality applications. Triangle mesh, which represents a triangulation of the 3D model surface, is one of the most popular representations for 3D geometric models. This representation is ideal for hardware-assisted rendering with current graphics architectures. However, the increasing size of the geometric models consumes enormous network/computing/storage bandwidth and starts to exceed the capability of existing graphics systems. One solution to this bandwidth problem is to apply lossless compression algorithms to triangle mesh. In this research, we present a simple and efficient lossless triangle mesh compression algorithm based on a novel way of compactly encoding the connectivity information through a breadth-first traversal of the input mesh, and hence the name BFT, which can be directly processed by the 3D graphics applications as well as by the polygonal rendering hardware.
